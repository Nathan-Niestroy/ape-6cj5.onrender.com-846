Metrics Section

Metrics used to determine a student's success for a role

Currently

  • Past work experience
    Issue: Usually, minimal years of experience and the issue of identifying what qualifies as relevant experience.
  • Determining a candidate by their network
    Issue: Students are starting in their industry and besides professors and personal friends are likely to have no career connections.
  • Recruiting at Job Fair
    Issue: Job fairs cost time and money with a recruiter representing your company to hundreds of students. Often these events can feel very forced, and candidates may struggle in showing their best qualities in a 2–3-minute conversation.
  • Previous Clubs and Organizations
    Issue: While clubs and organizations can be valuable for building connections, their size and relevance often vary depending on the field and institution. Finding a club that directly aligns with a specific industry is particularly challenging at community colleges or underrepresented colleges.

APE way

  • Previous company experience
    By participating in these projects, students gain valuable industry experience directly related to the work your company focuses on. Allowing you to assess a student’s technical fit for a position, providing a clear indication of their potential.
  • Personal Student Connections within the company
    Through these projects, you can build connections with the students who are most passionate about your company. This gives you the opportunity to learn more about their stories and motivations before the interview or hiring process begins.
  • Workshop events
    Our workshop events combine the benefits of traditional recruiting fairs with added value. Your recruiters can prepare students for the application process and have meaningful one-on-one interactions with interested candidates, without the pressure of discussing job-specific questions.
  • Partnering with APE to provide projects
    Our program is a fraction of the cost of other programs. Additionally, for most networking events, we provide sponsored travel for select partners.

The Problem

Through our research of the job market, we found that many engineering students do not secure internships or industry-related job positions before graduating with their bachelor’s degree.

From the company’s perspective, finding the right candidate requires significant effort to ensure a good fit for both the present and future. Large companies receive numerous applications each semester, and an applicant's submission can be dismissed in mere seconds.

Beyond recruitment, dedicating time to train and develop interns often takes away from important projects or goals, especially when many students are unsure of how to make the most of their internship experience.

Our Solution

Rather than focusing on simply providing more jobs or personally recruiting to ensure companies receive the right candidates, we empower students and companies to collaborate in developing the skills and experience necessary for a student's first job.

Our projects are designed to address common onboarding challenges, employment fit, and other issues typically encountered when hiring new talent. By making diverse work opportunities available to students, we tackle the issue of student development head-on.

As a student development program that introduces students to the workplace, we emphasize the training of professionalism and interpersonal skills alongside technical expertise. Instead of relying solely on student organizations and personal networks to build teamwork abilities, our program offers a structured workshop environment where essential skills like teamwork, innovation, and problem-solving are cultivated. We also bring industry leaders into the conversation, addressing the challenges students face as they embark on their career journeys.

Through our initiative, we are creating a platform that directly prepares students for the workplace, equipping them with the experience, connections, and skills they need to begin and excel in their careers.
